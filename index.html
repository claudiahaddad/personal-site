About

I'm drawn to early, ambiguous spaces, and to the challenge of turning emerging primitives into products people actually understand and use.

I started my career as an engineer and still love building—whether that's shipping PRs, prototyping ideas, or organizing hackathons and helping others bring their ideas to life.

Across my work, I've launched 0→1 consumer products, built developer communities, and scaled products to millions of users.

I studied Cognitive Science at the University of Pennsylvania, with a focus on decision-making and consumer psychology. I think a lot about behavior, trust, and how to make complex systems feel simple, intuitive, and worth coming back to.

Outside of work, I spend a lot of time reading, planning trips to interesting places, and finding good coffee shops.
